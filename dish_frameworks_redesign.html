Dish Frameworks

Understanding the anatomy of great cooking

Deconstruct to Create

Every memorable dish follows a fundamental structure. By understanding these six core principles, you can deconstruct any recipe, identify what makes it work, and apply these insights to create your own culinary masterpieces with confidence and creative freedom.

1

Foundation

The structural base that provides substance and carries the dish. This is your canvas - the element that everything else builds upon and what creates the dish's fundamental character.

Examples:
Pasta, rice, bread, protein cuts, vegetable bases, stocks, or any primary ingredient that forms the dish's backbone
"The foundation must be executed perfectly, as it cannot be masked by other elements. It sets the standard for the entire dish."
2

Core Flavour

The dominant taste profile that defines the dish's identity. This is the primary flavor that someone would use to describe the dish - what makes it recognizable and memorable.

Examples:
Tomato in marinara, lemon in piccata, garlic in aglio e olio, mushroom in risotto, smoke in barbecue, or the defining spice blend in curries
"The core flavor should be clear, pronounced, and present in every bite. It's the flavor that makes someone say 'this tastes like...'"
3

Amplifying Ingredients

Supporting elements that enhance, complement, or contrast with the core flavor without overpowering it. These ingredients add complexity and depth to the primary taste.

Examples:
Onions and carrots in tomato sauce, herbs with lemon, wine with mushrooms, celery in stocks, or complementary spices that support the main flavor profile
"Amplifying ingredients should feel natural and harmonious. They support the star without demanding attention for themselves."
4

Unifying Agents

Elements that bind the dish together, creating cohesion between all components. These ensure the dish feels integrated rather than like separate ingredients on a plate.

Examples:
Butter and cheese in pasta, pan juices and reductions, emulsions like mayonnaise, cooking liquids that absorb flavors, or fats that carry and meld tastes
"Unifying agents create the magic moment when separate ingredients become a single, cohesive dish. They're often what separates good cooking from great cooking."
5

Finishing Elements

Final touches that provide contrast, freshness, texture, or visual appeal. These elements elevate the dish from complete to exceptional.

Examples:
Fresh herbs, citrus zest, flaky salt, cracked pepper, nuts for crunch, microgreens for color, or any element added at the very end
"Finishing elements should provide a moment of surprise or delight. They're the difference between a dish that's good and one that's memorable."
6

The Governing Principles

The underlying rules and techniques that ensure all elements work in harmony. These are the invisible forces that make or break a dish's success.

Examples:
Proper seasoning balance, temperature control, timing of additions, texture contrasts, color balance, and portion relationships between components
"Governing principles are what separate cooking from chemistry. They transform a list of ingredients into a harmonious, delicious experience."
